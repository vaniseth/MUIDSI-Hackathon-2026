EMERGENCY CONTACTS
================================================================================

IMMEDIATE DANGER - CALL 911
Use for: Medical emergencies, fire, violent crimes in progress, active threats, any life-threatening situation.

MU Police Department (MUPD): 573-882-7201
Available 24/7. Use for: Non-emergency safety concerns, suspicious activity, crime reporting, escort requests.
Website: https://mupolice.missouri.edu/

Friend Walk (Walking Escort): 573-884-9255
Hours: Sunday-Thursday, 7:00 PM - 3:00 AM
Free walking escort service anywhere on campus. Call when uncomfortable walking alone.
Also available via Mizzou Safe app.

Safe Ride (Campus Shuttle): 573-882-1010
Hours: Daily, 7:00 PM - 3:00 AM
Free shuttle service across campus. Wait time usually 10-15 minutes.

MU Alert - Emergency Notification System
Sign up at: https://mupolice.missouri.edu/services/mu-alert/
MU Alert sends emergency texts, emails, and desktop notifications during campus threats, severe weather, or active incidents. Sign up immediately.

Mizzou Safe App
Download the Mizzou Safe app to: call MUPD, request Friend Walk, report tips, and access safety resources directly from your phone.

REPORTING A CRIME OR INCIDENT - OFFICIAL FORMS & LINKS
================================================================================

1. CAMPUS SECURITY AUTHORITY (CSA) INCIDENT REPORTING FORM
   Use when: You are a Campus Security Authority and witnessed or were told about a Clery Act crime.
   Form: https://missouri.qualtrics.com/jfe/form/SV_1THwCQ2fvSs1SzH
   Required by: Jeanne Clery Campus Safety Act

2. ONLINE CRIME REPORT (MUPD)
   Use for: Lost property, theft, theft from vehicle, vandalism, vandalism to a vehicle.
   Report at: https://mupolice.missouri.edu/report-crime/
   Note: For emergencies or crimes in progress, call 911 or 573-882-7201 instead.

3. SILENT WITNESS - ANONYMOUS REPORTING
   Use when: You want to report a crime or suspicious activity anonymously.
   Report at: https://mupolice.missouri.edu/about/units/investigations/
   Note: MUPD receives your report but not your name or location.

4. OFFICE OF INSTITUTIONAL EQUITY (OIE) - DISCRIMINATION & TITLE IX REPORT
   Use for: Sexual assault, sexual harassment, dating/domestic violence, stalking, discrimination, retaliation, or bias incidents.
   Report at: https://equity.missouri.edu/reporting-and-policies/
   Phone: 573-882-3880
   Email: equity@missouri.edu
   Location: 320 Jesse Hall and 145 Heinkel Building
   Note: MU employees are Mandated Reporters and must report to OIE.

5. STUDENT AT RISK / CARE TEAM REPORT
   Use when: You are concerned about a student who is distressed, displaying harmful behavior, or needs support (financial hardship, mental health, housing insecurity, etc.).
   Report at: https://studentaffairs.missouri.edu/concerned-about-a-student/
   Phone: Dean of Students Office: 573-882-5397
   Note: If student is an immediate threat, call MUPD at 573-882-7201 instead.

6. COLUMBIA CRIME STOPPERS - ANONYMOUS TIP LINE
   Use when: You have information about a crime and want to remain anonymous.
   Phone: 573-875-TIPS (8477)
   No name required. May be eligible for reward money.

SEXUAL ASSAULT & INTERPERSONAL VIOLENCE RESOURCES
================================================================================

RSVP CENTER (Relationship & Sexual Violence Prevention)
Services: FREE and CONFIDENTIAL crisis support, case management, advocacy.
RSVP staff are exempt from mandated reporting - your information stays with them.
Location: G216 MU Student Center
Phone: 573-882-6638
Email: rsvp@missouri.edu
Hours: Monday-Friday, 8 AM - 4 PM
Website: https://rsvp.missouri.edu/

TRUE NORTH - Domestic & Sexual Violence Services
Services: Comprehensive support for survivors of intimate partner violence and sexual assault.
Phone: 573-875-1369
Website: https://www.truenorthcolumbia.org/

UNIVERSITY HOSPITAL S.A.N.E. CLINIC
Services: Sexual Assault Nurse Examiners (SANE) conduct exams following sexual assault/rape. Evidence collected can be preserved even if you are not ready to report.

If you experience sexual assault:
1. Get to a safe place
2. Call 911 or MUPD if in immediate danger
3. Contact RSVP Center: 573-882-6638 (confidential)
4. Consider medical attention at University Hospital SANE Clinic
5. Preserve evidence if possible (do not shower, change clothes)
6. Report to OIE (optional but supported): https://equity.missouri.edu/reporting-and-policies/
7. Report to MUPD (optional): 573-882-7201

================================================================================
REPORTING SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY
================================================================================

If you observe suspicious activity:
1. Trust your instincts
2. Note details: person description, vehicle, location, time
3. Call MUPD at 573-882-7201 or use Silent Witness for anonymity
4. Do not confront the individual
5. Move to a well-lit, populated area

Examples include: people trying door handles, loitering without purpose, unattended bags or packages, individuals following others.

PERSONAL SAFETY TIPS
================================================================================

Walking on campus:
- Stay in well-lit areas, especially at night
- Use Friend Walk or Safe Ride after dark
- Keep your phone charged and accessible
- Be aware of your surroundings - avoid headphones at max volume
- Trust your instincts
- Use emergency blue light phones if needed

Dorm and housing safety:
- Always lock your door, even briefly
- Do not prop exterior doors open
- Report strangers in your building to RA or MUPD
- Do not share entry codes or swipe access
- Register your bike and electronics with MUPD: https://mupolice.missouri.edu/services/bicycle-registration/

Digital safety:
- Register valuables (electronics, bike) with MUPD
- Keep valuables out of plain view in your car
- Lock your car at all times

CLERY ACT & CAMPUS SECURITY AUTHORITIES
================================================================================

The Jeanne Clery Campus Safety Act requires MU to:
- Maintain a public daily crime log
- Issue timely warnings for serious threats
- Publish annual crime statistics
- Train Campus Security Authorities (CSAs)

CSA Incident Report Form: https://missouri.qualtrics.com/jfe/form/SV_1THwCQ2fvSs1SzH
CSA Training (Percipio): Log in to Percipio, look for "2024 Campus Security Authority Training" under assignments.
