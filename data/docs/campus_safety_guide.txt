MU CAMPUS SAFETY GUIDE - Quick Reference

EMERGENCY CONTACTS

Call 911 for all emergencies including:
- Medical emergencies
- Fire or smoke
- Violent crimes in progress
- Any immediate danger situation

MU Police Department (MUPD): 573-882-7201
Available 24/7 for campus safety concerns, crime reporting, and non-emergency assistance.

REPORTING SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY

If you observe suspicious activity:
1. Trust your instincts
2. Note details: person description, vehicle, location, time
3. Call MUPD at 573-882-7201 immediately
4. Do not confront the individual
5. Move to a safe, well-lit, populated area

Examples of suspicious activity include people trying door handles, loitering in unusual places, taking photos of security measures, or unattended packages.

LATE NIGHT SAFETY SERVICES

Friend Walk: 573-884-9255
Hours: Sunday-Thursday, 7:00 PM - 3:00 AM
Free walking escort service anywhere on campus. Call when you feel uncomfortable walking alone.

Safe Ride: 573-882-1010
Hours: Daily, 7:00 PM - 3:00 AM
Free shuttle service across campus. Wait time is usually 10-15 minutes.

PERSONAL SAFETY TIPS

When walking on campus:
- Stay in well-lit areas
- Walk with friends when possible
- Keep phone charged and accessible
- Be aware of surroundings
- Trust your instincts
- Use emergency blue light phones if needed
- Call Friend Walk if alone at night

Dorm safety:
- Always lock your door
- Don't prop doors open
- Report strangers in building
- Don't share entry codes
- Use peephole before opening door

SEXUAL ASSAULT RESOURCES

Title IX Office: 573-882-3880
Confidential support for sexual harassment, assault, dating violence, and stalking.

24/7 Hotline: 573-882-6638
Relationship & Sexual Violence Prevention Center

If you experience sexual assault:
1. Get to safe place
2. Consider medical attention
3. Preserve evidence if possible
4. Contact someone you trust
5. Call Title IX: 573-882-3880
6. Can report to MUPD: 573-882-7201

CRIME REPORTING

Report crimes by:
- Calling MUPD: 573-882-7201
- Online: https://mupd.missouri.edu/report-crime/
- In person at MUPD Office
- Anonymous tips online

When reporting, provide: what happened, when, where, who was involved, witnesses, and any evidence.

Report within 24 hours for best results.

IMPORTANT REMINDERS

- Your safety is the top priority
- Trust your instincts
- Don't hesitate to call for help
- Use available resources
- Look out for fellow students
- Report all crimes and suspicious activity

For more information: police.missouri.edu
